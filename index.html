Natural Resource Biometric Lab

The Natural Resource Biometrics Lab at Auburn University is a research lab focused on the application of quantitative methods in the field of forest biometrics. We specialize in forest biometrics, spatial statistics, and forest ecology. We provide research, training, and consulting services.

Current Research

Invasive tree species

Chinese tallow is an invasive tree species introduced to the US in the 18th century. With a very high reproductive rate, the uncontrolled spread has proven problematic in Southeastern states. Work is being done to study its spread and control.
